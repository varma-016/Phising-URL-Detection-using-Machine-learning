      Phishing Safety Guide

      If you click a phishing link, use these steps immediately to reduce risk and report the incident.

      How To Recover Quickly

      • Disconnect from the suspicious site and close the page immediately.
      • Change passwords for affected accounts first, then enable 2-factor authentication.
      • Sign out from all active sessions in account security settings.
      • Run a full anti-virus or anti-malware scan on your device.
      • If banking details were shared, contact your bank and block cards right away.

      How To Protect From Phishing

      • Always verify domain spelling before login, payment, or OTP actions.
      • Do not trust urgent messages asking for password, OTP, or payment confirmation.
      • Type important websites manually instead of opening unknown links.
      • Use strong unique passwords with a password manager.
      • Keep browser, OS, and security software updated regularly.

      How To Raise A Complaint

      • Collect evidence: screenshot, full URL, message/email sender, and timestamp.
      • Report to your bank/payment provider if money, card, or UPI details were shared.
      • Report to your national cybercrime portal or local police cyber cell.
      • In India, use cybercrime.gov.in and call 1930 for financial fraud support.
      • Report phishing emails in your mail provider ("Report phishing") to block future attacks.
